Food and Drink Policy

Westward School

Food and Drink Policy

EYFS

Rationale

In the Early Years at Westward we believe it is essential to provide children with positive healthy eating experiences in order to promote their wellbeing. We also encourage children to look at the long-term effects of a healthy and balanced diet.

We respect the different dietary, cultural and health needs of all our children.

Implementation of the policy in the Early Years at Westward

The proprietor, Head teacher and senior management team must ensure that:

  • new members of staff, including students and volunteers, receive copies of the Food and Drink Policy or Manual, and that this is discussed with them during their induction meeting.
  • all staff, students and volunteers are made fully aware of individual children’s dietary needs and requirements.
  • the Food and Drink Policy is made available to all parent and carers.

 

Availability of water

  • We have fresh drinking water and suitable cups, or water from the outside fountains or water bottles readily available for children and staff at all times.
  • We encourage the children to help themselves to water, either with support or independently.
  • We support the children in recognising that they need to drink water when they are thirsty, hot or tired, or feeling unwell.
  • We monitor the amounts children drink.

 

Meals

  • We provide varied, healthy, regular and adequate meals.

 

Before the children come into the hall for lunch;

  • Tables are put out and cleaned by staff on the rota at midday.
  • Nursery table mats and coloured plastic cutlery are set out on the table.
  • Kitchen staff prepare and set out food onto the counter ready to be served in accordance with food safety and hygiene guidelines.
  • The Cook prepares the Nursery children’s plates of food and is fully aware of any child’s intolerance of particular foods.
  • Each child has a small portion of all the days choices on their plate plus a glass of water.
  • Plates are placed on the Nursery table ready as they come into the hall.

 

Nursery Children

  • Are taken to the toilets to wash their hands before entering the lunch hall.
  • They walk quietly in a line into the lunch hall and choose where they are going to sit down.
  • An adult on duty helps to arrange where the children are sitting.
  • The Nursery children sit on small plastic chairs under low tables in the hall appropriate to their age and height.
  • Children all put hands together and say grace.
  • After grace the children can start to eat their lunch. Followed by a dessert and fruit.
  • Members of staff on duty sit with the children while eating their lunch.
  • The Nursery children have to wait until everyone has finished lunch before they leave the table.
  • The adults on duty will take the children to the toilet and then back to the Nursery room after lunch ready for the afternoon session.

 

 

 

Kindergarten

  • Are taken to the toilet to wash their hands before lunch.
  • They line up quietly outside the hall with Year 1 and 2 children and wait until they are called into the hall by a lunchtime supervisor to sit down.
  • The children walk into the hall quietly and sit down on the benches at higher tables.
  • Kindergarten have set places to eat their lunch in the hall.
  • They sit with their peer group and have a year 2 monitor at the end of their table who helps them if needed, to cut their food, offer them dessert and fruit and glasses of water.
  • Mrs James or a lunchtime supervisor oversees all the children and they know to put up their hand and ask for her help if needed.
  • Mrs James and the lunchtime supervisors encourage the children to eat enough healthy choices for a satisfying diet, praises the children for eating well and showing good table manners. She also liaises with parents regarding the children’s eating habits as this communication is vital in allaying any fears a parent may have concerning their child’s eating arrangements.
  • The children use metal cutlery that is already set out for them on the table.
  • Children all put their hands together to say grace.
  • The children wait until they are called up to the counter, a table at a time to choose their food.
  • When the children are called up to the counter they first pick up a plate and then slowly move along the counter and talk to the cook and her assistant and ask for the food they would like saying please and thank you.
  • Mrs Johnson and her assistant encourage the children to choose a well balanced diet.
  • When they have selected their food they carry their plate back to the table to eat it.
  • The children can ask their monitor for water at any time during their meal.
  • The monitor helps the children to put their dirty plates, knives and forks away and then offers them a choice of puddings to eat.
  • The children wait until everyone is finished eating before they leave the hall..
  • No child will be allowed to leave the lunch hall unless they have been seen to eat a substantial amount of food.
  • Kindergarten go out to play in the large playground with Mrs Hannon after leaving the lunch hall.

 

At The End of Lunch

  • Food is cleared away and stored appropriately following the kitchen safety and hygiene procedures.
  • Tables are cleaned and stacked away safely.
  • The hall floor is swept and mopped.

 

Procedure for children who do not enjoy mealtimes

Mealtimes are an exciting time for most children during the school day but for some children they can find it a daunting experience and need extra support and encouragement.

 

If a member of staff has concerns about a child during mealtimes we will;

  • Talk to the parents/carers at the end of the session or day.

 

  • Or write a message into the home message book.

 

  • Discuss with the parents/carers a plan of action to support their child.

We will employ a variety of different strategies to help a child settle at mealtimes

 

Listed below are some useful strategies

  • The cook will prepare a special plate for the child of their favourite food if required daily.

 

  • We will then slowly introduce new foods to the child.

 

  • We will allow the child to choose to sit next to a friend.

 

  • A member of staff will sit next to the child and encourage them to join in mealtimes until they are settled.

 

  • We will use stickers as a reward when a child tries new things or begins to show more independence.

 

  • We will involve the child in menu planning and get them to select food they would like to eat.

 

  • We will talk about mealtimes during circle time and discuss likes/dislikes, trying new things, good behaviour, helping others, good manners and becoming independent.

 

Multi-cultural requirements

  • Staff respect and make arrangements for children’s cultural and religious needs.

Special dietary needs and food allergies

  • We use sensitivity in catering for children with specific dietary needs.
  • We update records of children’s specific dietary requirements regularly and keep these in a prominent place on Display in the front of each teachers planning file.

Snacks

  • We offer a selection of different fruit and vegetables at daily snack time, providing a range of cultural and nutritious experiences for the children.

 

At Westward we view snack time as an important part of the day where lots of personal, social and emotional development takes place.

 

  • It helps children gain independence
  • Children will learn to share and take turns in practical life skills
  • Children have access to food and drink when they want it
  • Good social interaction and discussion between peers and adults is promoted
  • Children are encouraged to display good manners and use the words please and thank you.

 

Snack produce

  • Children in Nursery and Kindergarten bring in a donation of fruit or vegetables during the week.
  • We encourage parents to get their child to choose their own fruit or vegetables to bring in, to involve them in the planning.

 

Snack time procedure

  • One adult is in charge of the snack area each day.
  • We encourage parents to volunteer to help run the snack area. Please see Mrs Stevenson if you are interested in helping out. All volunteers must adhere to the schools’ volunteer policy and undergo a CRB check.
  • 9.30 – 10.00 – Snack preparation time
  • Children who wish to come and prepare the snack for the rest of the Early Years can do so.
  • The children wash their hands, dry them and put on a plastic apron.
  • The children sit down and choose what they would like to prepare.
  • The adult in charge assists the children in preparing the fruit and vegetables.
  • The children put the prepared produce onto a large serving platter in the middle of the table.
  • Enough fruit and vegetables is prepared for all the children.
  • When the fruit has been prepared the adult in charge washes the table down ready for the snack area to open.

10am onwards – Snack area opens

The children follow the seven step procedure below.

  1. They look to see if there is a space at the table.
  2. Then they find their name and put it in the pot.
  3. Next they wash and dry their hands.
  4. Then they find their mat and take a cup and sit down at the table.
  5. They pour themselves a drink and choose some fruit and vegetables to eat.
  6. When they have finished they put their cup and mat in the washing up box.
  7. Finally they wash their hands before returning to play.

 

Policy for children arriving at school who have not eaten breakfast

 

In the event that a child arrives at school and says that they are hungry because they have not had any breakfast, the teacher will inform Mrs James the School Manager, who will give the child a bowl of cereal or a piece of fruit to eat from the breakfast club supplies.

 

The School Manager or a member of staff will speak to the parents at the end of the day or write a message in the home message book to inform them that their child was hungry when they arrived at school and that eating a breakfast is very important to start the day.

 

We will reinforce the message about the importance of eating a breakfast during class circle times, assemblies and parent information meetings.

 

Storage of food

  • Food is stored in hygienic and clean airtight containers.
  • Food that needs to be kept chilled is put on the appropriate shelf in a fridge.
  • Food is always eaten by its sell-by date
