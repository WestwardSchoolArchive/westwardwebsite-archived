UPPER SCHOOL DOES ‘GANGSTA GRANNY’

 

Friday 16th September dawned wet and miserable, but the children in the Upper School were anything but miserable, as they were off to Woking to watch David Walliams' ‘Gangsta Granny’.

 

After an uneventful trip to the venue we expectantly waited for the play to begin and we weren’t to be disappointed. The show was excellent and the children sat spellbound throughout. The scenery was stunning and the acting excellent. There was lots of humour which was really appreciated by all in the auditorium. There was plenty of audience participation, which kept all involved.

 

Hopefully, the children learned from the experience and this will spark creativity in their writing and acting. The story had many morals, one of which was that is wrong to steal and that Cabbage Soup isn’t too bad.


A big thank you to the bus company for getting us safely to and from Woking. An even bigger thank you to Mrs Williams for organising such a wonderful morning out.
