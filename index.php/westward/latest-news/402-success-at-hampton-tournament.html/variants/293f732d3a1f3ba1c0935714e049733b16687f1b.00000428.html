SUCCESS AT HAMPTON TOURNAMENT

 

On Saturday 10th September the U10 and U11 Westward football teams took part in the Hampton 5-a-side Football Tournament.

 

 

Both teams had a tough programme for the day with the U11s playing seven games and the U10s eleven games without a break. Most of our opponents were schools a lot larger than Westward and added to the tough opposition was the rather wet weather.

 

Mr Sloggett had kindly accepted responsibility for the U10s and they were soon underway. Their first game was against a strong Twickenham Prep side. The boys played well, winning a tough game. This set the tone for the next four games, which we also won. A little dip in the middle of the tournament led to us losing games that we should have won, but we fought back from this and won the next couple of games comfortably, ending a very successful tournament with a  0 - 0 draw in the final game.

 

The boys’ effort and great teamwork paid off as they were named runners-up in the Year 5 category. Well done to all the players on a very good start to the season. The player of the tournament was deliberated over for a long while and in the end it was decided, due to such a good team effort, the entire team should be awarded with Player of the Tournament.

 

The U11 team, most of whom had played in the tournament before, were up against a tough Sheen Mount School team. The boys dominated the early encounters and should have scored more goals. In the end we managed a creditable 2 - 2 draw, both goals being scored by Ben. Jamie had a good game in defence.

 

Our next game was against a very strong Durston House team. We were up against it and eventually lost 0 - 3. Toby had a solid game in defence. We were soon back to winning ways against St. James’ Catholic School, winning 5 - 2 with four goals from Ben and one from Kristian. Hampton Prep were our next opposition, who we thought would be really tough. We were eventually 4 - 0 winners, with three goals from Ben and one from Kristian. Kristian kept on the score sheet against Priory Prep where the team drew 1 - 1. Jamie had an outstanding game in goal. The game against Newland House was another 1 - 1 draw, with Tommy getting his name onto the score sheet. Our last game saw a tired team take on Esher CE Primary which we lost 0 - 4.

 

The team played well throughout and deserved the successes they had. Jamie played well in defence and Toby had solid games at the back. However, Ben with his nine goals was Player of the tournament.

 

Our thanks go to Hampton for another well run tournament. It was great to see Mrs Stevenson supporting the boys with all of the parents who came along to brave the weather. Thank you all for your support.

 

Mr Pettipher
