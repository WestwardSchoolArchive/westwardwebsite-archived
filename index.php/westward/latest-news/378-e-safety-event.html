E-SAFETY AT WESTWARD

 

On Wednesday 10th February Westward was visited by two very good speakers from the Lucy Faithfull Foundation to talk about the very important subject of E-Safety.

 

The children regularly have E-Safety lessons within the computing curriculum and we thought it would be a good idea for the older children on this occasion to hear about the importance of E-Safety from an outside speaker.

 

The Year 4 and 5 session consisted of a very good Power Point display and a very hard hitting video on cyberbullying with the Year 6 session exploring the subject in more depth with an additional video containing E-Safety content linked to their age group. The main theme of each session was to ensure that our children are staying safe online. There were some very important tips and advice, which hopefully our children will heed.

 

At 16.00 the parents and staff were put through their paces on the same subject. Again, we watched a couple of hard hitting videos and discussed the various problems, parents, staff and children could face online. All in all, the talks to the children and the adults were most informative, educating and well presented.

 

It was a great pity that the parent session was so poorly attended, particularly as E-safety is such an important aspect of modern society and that parents are in fact at the coal front in this ‘battle’.

 

A very big thank you to the ladies from The Lucy Faithfull Foundation.

 

 

Mr Pettipher

E-Safety Officer
