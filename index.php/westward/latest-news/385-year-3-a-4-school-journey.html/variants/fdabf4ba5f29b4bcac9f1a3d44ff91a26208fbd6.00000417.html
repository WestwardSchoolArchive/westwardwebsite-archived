YEAR 3 & 4 SCHOOL JOURNEY

 

The day dawned wet and miserable, but this didn’t dampen the spirits of thirty nine year 3’s & 4’s excitement and enthusiasm - they were off to New Barn in Dorset.

 

With the coach packed a fond farewell was waved to parents and the school and the adventure was beginning. We had an uneventful trip to our lunch break site at Avon Heath near Ringwood. With the weather very wet we were limited as to where we could eat our lunch. Thankfully the activity centre came to our rescue and allowed us to use their facilities. We were soon back on our way after cleaning up after ourselves.

 

On time, New Barn beckoned us and we quickly unpacked and got ready for the first activity - Orienteering. Despite the drizzle the children had fun locating the various clues. The dinner of chicken nuggets was much appreciated by all and we were ready for the next exciting activity - shadow puppets.

 

We were all treated to six wonderfully entertaining puppet shows with the top puppeteers being adjudged to be: Ryan, Stephanie, Arthur, Tara, Ruaridh, Inaaya and Emily.

 

Bed time was relatively quick and soon Thursday dawned, a lovely sunny, calm day and we were ready for a fun filled day exploring Monkey World and trying our hand at Ten Pin Bowling.

 

We had a lovely time at Monkey World, really enjoyed their playground and spent lots of time and money in the shop. Bowling was great fun, with almost everyone getting in their allotted two games. The best bowlers were Carter for the boys and Phoebe for the girls. What a great day!

 

The day wasn’t over as we still had the legendary Pirate Shindig to look forward to. As usual the shindig lived up to expectations and a number of children walked away with prizes for various things - a superb end to a lovely day.

 

Always the worst part is the packing up to go home. We all eventually found our belongings and soon the bus was packed and ready to go but not before the prize giving. The winners, not already mentioned, were:

 

Best Dorm - Dorm 1 - Tom, Dan, Eashan

 

Children that benefited and contributed greatly

Yr 3 - Elsa

Yr 4 - Emily

 

Orienteering - Tom, Mario & Laurence

 

Birthdays - Ryan & Laurence

 

A very big thank you to all the staff - Ms Drislane, Mrs Williams, Mrs Humble & Mrs Harris who worked really hard to ensure the success of the trip and look after the children’s welfare. Thank you Ealson’s Transport for getting us to and from New Barn safely, and other parties who contributed to the success of the  trip - thank you!
